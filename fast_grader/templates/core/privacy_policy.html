Use of your Google Data

This application integrates with Google Classroom and Google Drive. By logging in and creating an account, you grant us permission to access data associated with your google account.

Your own personal information associated with your Google Account, like your name and email address, are stored in our secure database, and are never shared with third parties. Your username may be shared with third parties like LogRocket and similar analytics platforms for the sake of providing us knowledge about your use of the website and improving our platform.

The functionality of this application requires us to consume data from Google Classroom and Google Drive. In these cases, we only save the data that is necessary to the functionality of this website, like course names, assignment names, student names, your grades, and your comments. This data is stored in our database, but it always belongs to you. You have the right to delete this data at any time, and upon deleting the grading session from your profile home page, it is immediately, permanently, and irreversibly deleted from our database as well. Security and privacy when dealing with Classroom and Drive data, in particular, is of the utmost importance to us. Student names are protected by COPPA, and will always be securely stored and never shared.

Our use of LogRocket to analyze your usage of the grading tool is the only exception to this rule, but we use a find grain of detail in specifying exact which content on the page is removed from LogRocket's view. For example, (click to see source), inside the grading tool, we apply the data-private attribute to the student name on the page, which tells LogRocket to not record the content of this field, and therefore LogRocket cannot see the student names despite recording the remainder of the page.

LogRocket

LogRocket will be used to analyze your usage of the grading app and continuously improve it. Most of the content on the page, including grades, comments, and student submission content is captured by this tool. However, we have ensured that LogRocket will not capture any student names whatsoever, so any assignment submission data it does have visibility of will be anonymous. Children's Online Privacy Protection Rule ("COPPA")

This website is COPPA compliant, because any personal information about individuals under 13 years of age is provided by an adult who has the permission to do so. Any student information in our database (as defined under COPPA) is treated as sensitive data under the presumption that all students are potentially under thirteen years of age. Fast Grader uses industry standard security practices, including Transport Layer Security (TLS), and user authentication via Google.
